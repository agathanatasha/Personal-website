Comparing Word Choices Between News Wires of Different English Speaking Countries

15-19 Februrary 2016

Smart Data Hack, University of Edinburgh

Introduction

Curious about the linguistics differences between dialects of English beyond spelling differences, such as British English, Canadian English, American English, etc. With the massive amount of writings available online, it is the perfect mix of linguistics and computer science.

To simplify the project for a 5-day hack, I focused on word choices and frequencies, using term frequency-inverse document frequency (tf-idf) as a measure. News articles were used in the project. As a starting point, journalism genre is a cleaner form of writing in terms of spelling and sentence structure, while the quality of other forms of writing available online such as blogs, posts and tweets might vary from person to person. I picked CBC and BBC and "Zika virus" as the topic to further narrow down the amount of information.

Methodology

The project is completed solely in Python. I used the library "Beautiful Soup" for pulling data out of HTML files. Then I used the tf-idf vectorizer provided in scikit-learn library. Finally, the tf-idf information is passed onto a library named "wordcloud" for visualization.

Results

The code is published on github.
